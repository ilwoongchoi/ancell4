What Are
the Parts of the
Immune System?

Many cells and organs work together to protect the body. White blood cells,
also called leukocytes, play an important role in the immune system.
Some types of white blood cells, called phagocytes,
chew up invading organisms. Others, called lymphocytes,
help the body remember the invaders and destroy them.

One type of phagocyte is the neutrophil, which fights
bacteria. When someone might have bacterial infection, doctors can
order a blood test to see if it caused the body to have lots of
neutrophils. Other types of phagocytes do their own jobs to make sure
that the body responds to invaders.

The two kinds of lymphocytes are B lymphocytes and T
lymphocytes. Lymphocytes start out in the bone marrow and
either stay there and mature into B cells, or go to the thymus gland
to mature into T cells. B lymphocytes are like the body's military
intelligence system they find their targets and send defenses to lock
onto them. T cells are like the soldiers they destroy the invaders
that the intelligence system finds.
